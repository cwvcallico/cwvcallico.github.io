Projects

Emergent Commons Thesis

The redesign of Ruggles Station was driven by the relationship between programmed space and public space. The research phase of the project showed that the existing building provided a partially interiorized space that could be appropriated by all urban users. The new design improves upon this concept by forming strategic relationships between pedestrians, MBTA commuters, and Northeastern University students and staff to create an emergent public commons.

The Emergent Commons

The existing appropriation of Ruggles station is built on a complex set of users, resulting in a partially interiorized space that has the characteristics of a free and public open space, or a commons. These diagrams depict the social uses of the space, and the geographies and relationships they form.

The Urban Strategy

The project's massing carefully responds to and reinforces two primary urban moves, the Forsyth corridor that connects two halves of Northeastern's campus, and the MBTA Orange line track, which promotes commuter access to the area.

Boston Housing

The Boston housing project is a mix of housing types designed to revitalize a neighborhood fragmented by previous development decisions. A double loaded corridor combines with a row of townhouses to create density on one side while promoting the traditional neighborhood scale on the other.

Las Vegas Bike Shop

The historical development of Las Vegas, New Mexico, is closely tied to transportation. Privileged by its position along the Santa Fe Trail, and the AT+SF railroad, the city grew to a prominence. In the 1950's, interstate development drew traffic away from the city, slowing growth. The Bicycle Shop is imagined as the next step in Las Vegas' development story, transforming the railroad district nto a pedestrian-oriented neighborhood, centered by a public bike rental and repair shop, promoting infill.
